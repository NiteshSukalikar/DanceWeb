Kathak

The classical Indian dance form of Kathak originated in the northern regions of the country and is narrative in nature. The term 'Kathak' has been derived form the Sanskrit word 'Katha', which means a story. “Katha kahe so Kathak kahalave”


“Kathak” literally means a storyteller. Kathaka is the name of the community of musicians and dancers. These Kathakas wandered around the countryside narrating the mythological stories to the people through dance, music and poetry; all these arts are very closely associated with each other. This act of storytelling further established as an art form, which popularized as ‘Kathak.


Swift Pirouettes (Bhramaris), Vigorous footwork (Padanyas), elegant stances (Thaat), Graceful Gatnikas, Descriptive Gatbhavas and innovative use of bhav in abhinaya characterize Kathak. The sharp yet fluid movements synchronised with deft and brisk spins, unleashes a visual splendour onstage taking audiences on a spiritual journey.


Vandana, Utaan, Thaat, Amad, Tode-tukade, Kavitta, Tihaai, Paran, Chakkardar, Gatnikas, Gatbhava, Tatkar, Thumaries, Bhajans, Kajari, Hori, Tarana, Tilwat, Chaturang, Drupad, Ashtapadis are the components of Kathak.
